Cookies Policy Summary


CodeWithAJoyDas uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance user experience, maintain authentication sessions, and collect limited technical data that helps improve our services. This section explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your preferences.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser when you visit a website. They help websites remember information about your visit — such as login status, preferences, or analytics data — to provide a smoother and more personalized experience.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the basic operation of our website, such as maintaining your login session, authentication, and security. Disabling these may affect core functionality.
  • Preference Cookies: Used to remember your settings, such as theme, language, or interface preferences, so you don’t need to reconfigure them on each visit.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how users interact with our platform, allowing us to improve performance, fix issues, and optimize usability. These are anonymous and do not identify individual users.
  • Third-Party Cookies: Set by services integrated with our platform (such as Google Analytics or GitHub) to support login, analytics, or other functionality. These cookies are managed by their respective providers.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Keep you signed in during your session
  • Securely store your authentication data (e.g., GitHub or Google tokens)
  • Remember your display or editor preferences
  • Analyze usage trends to enhance user experience

4. Managing Cookies

You can control or delete cookies at any time through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or remove cookies, though doing so may affect certain features such as login persistence or personalization.

5. Third-Party Cookie Policies
